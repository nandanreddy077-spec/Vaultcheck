## New Outreach Angle: The "War on Fraud" Regulatory Urgency Play

*Based on Congressional action this week — 9 anti-fraud bills advanced, Trump executive order active*

The federal government has now explicitly entered the payment fraud conversation in a way that creates executive-level urgency. For CFOs, "the government is watching" is a different motivator than "fraud is expensive." It creates audit committee pressure, board-level attention, and a compliance-framing that makes the conversation about obligation rather than just risk.

This is a new angle because it shifts the frame from *risk management* (fear-based, reactive) to *compliance posture* (obligation-based, proactive). CFOs respond differently to obligation than to risk — risk they can accept, obligation they must address.

**New cold email angle: The "Regulatory Urgency" Play**

Subject line variant A (compliance framing):
> "Congress just advanced 9 anti-fraud bills. Your audit committee will ask about your AP controls."

Subject line variant B (direct urgency):
> "Trump signed an executive order to combat payment fraud. Are your vendor verification controls ready for the question?"

**Opening line (for CFOs at mid-market accounting firms and companies):**
> "Between the Nacha 2026 fraud monitoring rules that went live this year and the House Oversight Committee advancing 9 new anti-fraud bills last week, payment fraud controls are becoming an audit and governance question, not just a risk management one. If your AP process relies on manual vendor verification, you may be about to get asked about it by people with authority to act."

**Why this angle is different from previous outreach:**
- It doesn't rely on fear of being defrauded — it uses regulatory signal as external validation
- CFOs are conditioned to respond to "what will your audit committee ask" as an action-forcing question
- This framing has not appeared in competitor cold email campaigns — Trustpair and Trustmi are not using the Congressional news as an outreach angle
- It creates urgency based on timing ("last week," "this year") without fabricating a threat
- Natural progression: compliance concern → "what controls do we have?" → demo request

**Recommended sequence:** Send "Regulatory Urgency" subject line to CFO segment → follow up 3 days later with the Nacha 2026 compliance blog post link (once published) → third email 5 days later offers a 15-minute "AP fraud control gap review" call framed as an audit prep service.
