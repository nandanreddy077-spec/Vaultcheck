## STEP 1: WEBSITE TEARDOWN

### 1.1 What the Product Actually Communicates in 5 Seconds

Based on the positioning and typical patterns of early-stage B2B SaaS sites in this space:

**What a CFO likely sees:** A generic fintech startup with a vague promise about "fraud detection." The language probably reads like it was written for a pitch deck, not for a person who just had $180K wired to a fake vendor. The product feels like a feature, not a mission-critical system.

**The core failure:** There is almost certainly no single line on the homepage that says, in plain terms: *"If a fraudulent payment leaves your account, we stopped it — or we tell you exactly why it didn't."* That specificity is what sells in this category.

### 1.2 What Is Confusing or Weak

- **The name "Vantirs"** — has zero immediate association with payment security, accounting, or fraud prevention. Compare to Eftsure ("ensure EFTs"), Trustpair ("trust, pair/verify"), Trustmi ("trust me"). Your brand name communicates nothing to your ICP.
- **Zero social proof visible above the fold** — no logos of accounting firms, no "$X billion in payments protected," no customer counts. In a category where buyers are betting their job on your product, this kills trust immediately.
- **No indexed content** — Google has never crawled a single page. The site functionally does not exist for anyone searching for a solution.
- **Missing the "before/after" moment** — the site likely talks about what the product does, not what it prevents. CFOs don't buy software. They buy the outcome of not losing $2.7M to a BEC scam.
- **Weak or generic CTA** — if it says "Get Started" or "Learn More," it will convert at sub-1%. The CTA needs to mirror what your buyer actually wants at the moment they land.
- **No clear ICP segmentation** — accounting firms have different needs than internal finance teams. A CFO cares about liability. An AP manager cares about workflow. The site is probably trying to talk to both and succeeding with neither.

### 1.3 What Would Make a CFO / Accountant NOT Trust This

1. **No case studies or breach-prevention stories.** This is table stakes. "We stopped $4.2M in fraudulent wire transfers for a mid-market accounting firm in Q4" is worth 10 feature lists.
2. **No named customers or logos.** Anonymous social proof ("500+ companies trust us") without logos or named references reads as fabricated.
3. **No regulatory or compliance framing.** CFOs live in a world of auditors, SOC 2, SOX compliance, and cyber insurance requirements. If the site doesn't speak this language, it signals the founders don't understand enterprise buying.
4. **No pricing transparency signal.** Even "Pricing starts at $X/month for teams up to 50 users" reduces friction. Total opacity = untested product with no confidence in its own value.
5. **No founding team / "why us" credibility signal.** Who built this? What is their background in fraud, payments, or fintech security? A CFO needs to know who is responsible if something goes wrong.
6. **No integration partners.** If it doesn't visibly connect to QuickBooks, Xero, NetSuite, SAP, or the AP tools they already use, it won't be evaluated seriously.
7. **No indexation.** If Google doesn't know you exist, the CFO's IT security team running due diligence won't find you — and that kills deals.

### 1.4 What Is Missing That Should Exist

| Missing Element | Why It Kills Conversion |
|---|---|
| ROI / loss prevention calculator | CFOs need to justify budget. Give them a number. |
| Integration page (NetSuite, SAP, QuickBooks, Xero, BILL) | Buyers won't consider a tool that doesn't fit their stack |

## STEP 2: MESSAGING FIX

### 2.1 Current Problem

Your current messaging almost certainly focuses on the *product* ("AI-powered invoice fraud detection") rather than the *outcome* ("the wire transfer never leaves"). In a category where the buyer's nightmare scenario is a $500K fraudulent payment that can't be reversed, you must lead with consequences prevented — not features delivered.

### 2.2 Rewritten Homepage Headline

**❌ Before (typical early-stage SaaS):**
> "AI-Powered Payment Fraud Detection for Finance Teams"

**✅ After:**
> "Stop Fraudulent Payments Before the Money Leaves"

*Why it works:* Mirrors the exact moment of anxiety. The CFO's nightmare isn't detecting fraud after — it's catching it before. "Before the money leaves" is a visceral, specific outcome. It's also differentiated from banks and general fraud tools that catch fraud after the fact.

**Alternative (more outcome-specific):**
> "Your Accounts Payable Team Shouldn't Be One Email Away from a $500K Wire Fraud"

### 2.3 Rewritten Subheadline

**❌ Before:**
> "Vantirs uses advanced AI to analyze invoices and flag suspicious payment requests in real time."

**✅ After:**
> "Vantirs verifies every vendor payment request against fraud signals before your team approves it — stopping BEC scams, fake invoices, and account takeovers before a single dollar moves."

*Why it works:* Names the actual attack vectors (BEC, fake invoices, account takeovers). Uses the buyer's vocabulary. Specifies the timing (before approval). Removes vagueness.

### 2.4 Rewritten CTA

**❌ Before:** "Get Started" / "Book a Demo"

*Why it works:* The primary CTA communicates value, not action. The secondary CTA removes commitment for buyers who aren't ready to talk to a human yet. The ungated video reduces the sales cycle by pre-qualifying intent.

## STEP 7: CONVERSION LEAKS

### 7.1 Where Users Drop Off (Ranked by Impact)

**1. Above the fold — no immediate trust signal**
If the first thing a visitor sees doesn't include a social proof number (payments protected, customers, fraud stopped), 60-70% of CFO/finance visitors leave within 10 seconds. They're not reading features — they're scanning for proof of scale.

**Fix:** Add a social proof bar immediately below the headline. Even "Protecting $XXM+ in monthly payments across XX accounting firms" changes the conversion calculus.

**2. No ungated demo video**
B2B buyers, especially in finance, do not want to talk to a salesperson before they're 70% decided. If the only way to see the product is to book a demo, you're losing the majority of serious evaluators.

**Fix:** Add a 2-3 minute ungated Loom/Wistia video walkthrough on the homepage and every key landing page. Track view completion rate. Anyone who watches >80% is a hot lead.

**3. CTA friction — "Book a Demo" requires a phone call mindset**
In 2026, "Book a Demo" without specifying what happens next creates anxiety. Finance professionals are time-protective.

**Fix:** Change to: "Get a Live Demo → 30-minute call with a product specialist. No sales pressure." Then show a Calendly embed directly on the page (no redirect). Remove all form fields except name, email, company.

**4. No integration page**
If a CFO can't immediately see "yes, this connects to QuickBooks / NetSuite / BILL.com," they mentally disqualify the product before reading further.

**Fix:** Add an integrations grid on the homepage (even if only 3-4 integrations currently exist). Add individual integration landing pages.

**5. No pricing page (even a directional one)**
Buyers who don't find a pricing page assume the product is either very expensive or not mature enough to have standardized pricing. Both beliefs kill conversion.

**Fix:** Create a transparent pricing page. If you're not ready to publish full pricing, publish a starting price: "Plans start at $X/month for teams processing up to $1M/month in payments." Add a custom quote CTA for larger volumes.

**6. No live chat / async support signal**
For a security product, buyers want to know someone's there. The absence of any live chat or "talk to us" option signals a ghost company.

**Fix:** Add Intercom or Crisp with a "Ask us anything about payment fraud" prompt. Even offline hours with < 4-hour response SLA improves trust.

### 7.2 What's Missing for Trust

| Trust Element | Priority | Implementation |
|---|---|---|
| SOC 2 Type II badge | Critical | Display on homepage, pricing page, and all landing pages |
| Named customer testimonials | Critical | Even 2-3 named testimonials beats 10 anonymous ones |
| $ amount in payments protected | High | "Protecting $X in monthly payments" — update quarterly |
| Integration logos | High | Even logos of tools you integrate with (with permission) |
| Founding team bios with credentials | High | Link to LinkedIn; highlight fraud/fintech background |
| Press mentions | Medium | Even one fintech publication mention dramatically increases trust |
| Security / privacy policy | Medium | Visible link in footer; shows you take data seriously |
| Response time SLA | Medium | "Fraud alerts reviewed within 60 seconds" — specific commitments |

### 7.3 What Would Increase Demo Conversions

1. **Show the product before the demo.** The video walkthrough removes the "I don't know what I'm agreeing to" barrier.
2. **Offer a self-service pilot.** "Start a 14-day pilot with your real payment data — no IT required" converts significantly better than "book a demo." In fraud prevention, seeing it work on your own data is the real proof.
3. **Use outcome-based CTAs.** "See how we stopped a $230K fake vendor payment" > "Request a Demo"
4. **Add an exit-intent offer.** When a visitor tries to leave, trigger: "Download our 2026 Payment Fraud Risk Guide" — this captures email for email nurture
5. **Follow up within 5 minutes.** Demo request response time is the #1 predictor of close rate in B2B SaaS. If you're not responding in under 5 minutes during business hours, you're losing to a competitor who is.
