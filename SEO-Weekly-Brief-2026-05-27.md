## New Outreach Angle: The "Split Defenses" Opportunity

*Based on Trustmi's new webinar: "Split Defenses — How Fraud Slips Through Finance & Security Blind Spots"*

Trustmi's webinar framing is highly instructive: fraud exploits the gap *between* finance and IT security. Neither team catches it because each assumes the other has it covered. This is a real and widespread organizational pain that your ICP feels personally — especially at accounting firms where there is often *no* IT security team at all (it's just the accounting team and their AP software).

**New cold email angle: The "Two Teams" Problem**

This targets CFOs and accounting firm owners who have recently experienced a near-miss or breach, or who are aware of the problem but don't see it as *their* responsibility to solve.

**Subject line variant A (problem-aware):**
> "Your IT team thinks your AP team handles fraud. Your AP team thinks IT does."

**Subject line variant B (pattern interrupt):**
> "The $180K fraud that nobody caught — because two teams both assumed the other one was watching"

**Opening line (for CFOs at mid-market companies):**
> "Most payment fraud in 2026 doesn't break into your systems — it walks through the gap between your finance workflow and your email security tools. When we speak with CFOs, 8 out of 10 say they assume their AP team 'would catch it.' Their AP team assumes IT is monitoring. Both are wrong."

**Why this angle is different from previous outreach:**
- It doesn't lead with fear of a specific fraud type (BEC, fake invoices) — it leads with an organizational insight that feels true the moment a CFO reads it
- It's less threatening than "you're vulnerable to BEC" — it frames the problem as a structural gap, not a personal failure
- It creates an implicit question: "Wait, who *is* watching?" — which is exactly the question that leads to a demo request
- This angle hasn't appeared in competitor cold email campaigns (Trustpair and Trustmi use this framing in webinars but not outreach)

**Recommended sequence:** Send "Two Teams" subject line first → follow up 3 days later with the AI social engineering blog post (once published) → third email 5 days later references the AFP 76% stat and asks for a 15-minute call.
